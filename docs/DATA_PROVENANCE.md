**SOLUSDT funding, FTX-week off-schedule settlements (data-integrity
disclosure).** During the FTX crash week Binance ran SOLUSDT funding on a
~2h interval: 75 genuine off-schedule settlements between 2022-11-09 20:00
and 2022-11-18 06:00 UTC (stamps at 02/04/06/10/12/14/18/20/22, including
−2% cap prints). The widening recon characterized SOL's stamps as
millisecond-jittered only and missed this episode. The committed CSV keeps
the registered `funding_rate_8h` semantics: only ms-jittered stamps
(tolerance 60 s) snap to the 00/08/16 grid; the 75 off-schedule settlements
are EXCLUDED (snapping them would overwrite the genuine boundary rates).
The 00/08/16 series is verified hole-free through the episode — the
boundary settlements all occurred — so what is lost is only the extra
intra-window accrual on those nine days, during which the execution model
(registration §3) accrues funding at 00/08/16 stamps only. The backfill
script prints every excluded stamp on regeneration. BTC and ETH raw
histories were probed the same way: zero off-schedule settlements.

These files are small derived extracts of the providers' data, redistributed
solely for research reproducibility — so judges and readers can re-run the
pre-registered sweep without our infrastructure. All rights to the underlying
data remain with the respective providers (Binance, Bybit, Deribit,
CoinMarketCap). The one CoinGlass-relayed series we consulted was used ONLY
for the funding cross-check and is not committed as a dataset; we intentionally
document its 2025-04-08 storage-convention switch
(`data/backfill/funding_crosscheck.txt`) as data-integrity evidence — it is
exactly the mid-window source hazard the feature source-consistency rule
(R-SRC) exists to catch, and it is why the Lab uses Binance REST funding
end-to-end.
